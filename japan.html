The Japan Coffee Market

Japan is one of the world's most sophisticated and demanding coffee markets — and one of the most commercially significant. The country has been importing and consuming high-quality coffee for decades, developing a culture of precision, craftsmanship and connoisseurship that has shaped the global specialty coffee movement. Japanese buyers were among the first to pay premium prices for traceable, single-origin lots, and the country's influence on global standards for quality and presentation remains strong.

The coffee market encompasses everything from canned coffee at convenience stores to meticulously prepared pour-overs at world-class specialty cafés, with a robust middle market for quality espresso and filter coffee. For international suppliers, Japan offers access to buyers who understand and value quality — and who have the purchasing power to back it.

The Market Opportunity

Japan imports coffee from across the producing world, with strong demand for specialty and single-origin lots as well as equipment and accessories. The country's established network of importers and roasters provides well-defined routes to market for international suppliers, and trade events serve as the most efficient point of entry for companies without existing Japanese distribution.

While Japan requires patience and relationship-building compared to some markets, companies that invest in the relationship typically find durable, long-term commercial partnerships. A first meeting at a trade event, done well, can be the beginning of a multi-year supply relationship.
